# Demo plan — 2-3 minute walkthrough

## Script

### Cold open (0:00 – 0:15)
> "You have 30 days until the hackathon deadline. You have an idea. You don't have a brief."

Show the empty state of the app — clean gradient background, big textarea, gentle placeholder text.

### The one-tap flow (0:15 – 1:00)
1. Type slowly: *"An AI study buddy that turns lecture notes into flashcards."*
2. Click **Generate**.
3. Pause on the live visualization. The radial meter sweeps, sections appear one by one with subtle haptic-style feedback.
4. The complete brief renders in under 15 seconds.

### The reveal (1:00 – 1:30)
Scroll through the generated brief:
- **Name**: StudyForge
- **Tagline**: "Your lectures, turned into lasting knowledge."
- **Problem**: bullet points
- **Solution**: 2 paragraphs
- **Features**: 5 crisp items
- **Stack**: React, FastAPI, OpenAI, SQLite
- **Marketing**: a tweet-ready line

### Persistent memory (1:30 – 2:00)
1. Switch to the **Gallery** tab.
2. Generate a *second* project: *"A tool that helps remote teams run better standups."*
3. Show that the AI's suggestions are *informed* by the first project (different naming patterns, complementary features).
4. Click on a previous project — full memory recall.

### Export (2:00 – 2:20)
1. Open **Export** modal.
2. Show Markdown, HTML, and the **Shareable Card**.
3. Click *Download Shareable Card* — a polished PNG downloads.

### Dashboard close (2:20 – 2:40)
- Show the stats dashboard: 2 projects, 14 sections, 6 features generated, ~2 minutes saved.
- End on a clean shot of the dashboard with the tagline overlay.

### Call to action (2:40 – 3:00)
> "Devpost Companion. From idea to submission-ready in 30 seconds."

Logo + GitHub URL.

## Recording tips
- Use 1440p screen capture at 60fps.
- Record in a quiet room; the voiceover is the only audio.
- Use a mouse-jiggle script so the cursor doesn't disappear.
- Export at 1080p H.264 for Devpost's 200 MB cap.
